Startup Grant FAQs

What is non-dilutive funding for startups?

Non-dilutive funding is capital that does not require giving up equity in your company. This includes government grants, innovation prizes, accelerator stipends, and tax credits.

Can pre-revenue startups get grants?

Yes, many grant programs specifically target pre-revenue and early-stage startups. Programs like SBIR Phase I, IRAP, and Innovate UK grants are designed for startups still developing their products.

How much grant funding can a startup receive?

Startup grants range from $5,000 for local innovation prizes to over $2 million for federal research programs. Most startup-focused grants fall in the $25,000 to $500,000 range.

What do I need to apply for a startup grant?

Common requirements include a business plan, pitch deck, financial projections, team bios, and a description of the innovation or project.

Are startup grants taxable?

In most jurisdictions, grants received by for-profit startups are considered taxable income. However, the expenses funded by the grant may be deductible. Consult your accountant.
