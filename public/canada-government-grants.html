Federal Grant Programs

The Government of Canada offers extensive funding for innovation, exports, clean technology, and business growth through dozens of federal agencies.

NRC IRAP

The Industrial Research Assistance Program funds technology innovation projects for Canadian SMEs with up to $10M in non-repayable contributions.

SR&ED Tax Credits

Canada's largest R&D support program provides tax credits up to 35% on eligible research and development expenditures for CCPCs.

SDTC / Net Zero Accelerator

Clean technology funding through Sustainable Development Technology Canada and the $8B Net Zero Accelerator for decarbonization projects.

Frequently Asked Questions

What government grants are available for Canadian businesses?

Canada offers hundreds of federal and provincial grants including IRAP for technology innovation, SDTC for clean technology, SR&ED tax credits for R&D, CanExport for international expansion, and numerous provincial programs like Ontario's OVIN and BC's Innovator Skills Initiative.

How do I apply for a Canadian government grant?

Most Canadian government grants require you to create an account on the funding agency's portal, complete an eligibility assessment, prepare a detailed project proposal with budget, and submit before the deadline. GrantRadar helps you identify which grants you qualify for before you invest time applying.

What is the SR&ED tax credit program?

The Scientific Research and Experimental Development (SR&ED) program is Canada's largest single source of federal support for R&D. It provides tax credits of up to 35% on eligible R&D expenditures for Canadian-controlled private corporations, covering wages, materials, and contractor costs.

Can startups apply for Canadian government grants?

Yes, many Canadian programs specifically target startups including NRC IRAP for technology startups, Mitacs for research partnerships, and provincial programs like Alberta Innovates and MaRS Discovery District in Ontario. Some require a minimum operating history of 1-2 years.

Are Canadian government grants non-repayable?

Most Canadian government grants are non-repayable contributions, meaning you do not need to pay the money back. However, some programs like BDC and EDC offer repayable contributions or low-interest loans. GrantRadar clearly labels each program's funding type so you know what to expect.
