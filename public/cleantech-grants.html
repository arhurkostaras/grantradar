Cleantech Funding by Technology Area

Clean energy is the most heavily funded sector for government grants. Every major economy is investing billions in the transition to net zero.

Renewable Energy

Solar, wind, geothermal, and tidal energy projects. Grants for R&D, demonstration, and deployment of renewable generation and grid integration.

Energy Storage & Hydrogen

Battery technology, green hydrogen production, fuel cells, and long-duration storage solutions. High-priority funding area globally.

Carbon Capture & Industrial

CCUS technologies, industrial decarbonization, and process electrification. Growing funding pool for hard-to-abate sectors.

Electric Vehicles

EV manufacturing, charging infrastructure, battery recycling, and fleet electrification grants from federal and state programs.

Building Efficiency

Smart building technology, heat pumps, insulation innovation, and net-zero building design. Retrofit and new construction funding.

Circular Economy

Waste reduction, material recycling, sustainable packaging, and cradle-to-cradle design. Growing funding focus across all major economies.

Frequently Asked Questions

What grants are available for clean energy and cleantech companies?

Cleantech companies can access substantial funding including the DOE ARPA-E program (USA), Canada's Net Zero Accelerator ($8B), Innovate UK Energy Catalyst, EU Horizon Europe Clean Energy grants, and Australia's ARENA funding. The clean energy sector receives more grant funding than almost any other industry.

How much funding is available for cleantech grants?

Global cleantech grant funding exceeds $50 billion annually. Individual grants range from $50K for early-stage innovation to $100M+ for large infrastructure projects. The US Inflation Reduction Act alone allocated $370 billion for clean energy.

What types of cleantech projects qualify for grants?

Eligible projects include renewable energy development, battery and energy storage, carbon capture, electric vehicles and charging, building efficiency, industrial decarbonization, circular economy solutions, sustainable agriculture, and water treatment technologies.

Can cleantech startups get grants?

Yes, cleantech is one of the best-funded sectors for startups. SBIR/STTR programs at DOE fund early-stage innovation. Canada's SDTC and IRAP support cleantech startups. The EU's EIC Accelerator provides up to 2.5M euro in grants.

What is ARPA-E and how do I apply?

ARPA-E funds high-risk, high-reward energy technologies at the US Department of Energy. It issues focused funding opportunities with awards typically $500K to $10M. Applications are submitted through the ARPA-E eXCHANGE portal when solicitations are open.
