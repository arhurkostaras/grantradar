Government Programs for Indigenous Entrepreneurs

Federal and provincial governments across Canada, the USA, Australia, and New Zealand offer dedicated funding for Indigenous business owners.

Aboriginal Entrepreneurship Program

Non-repayable contributions up to $99,999 through Aboriginal Financial Institutions for Indigenous business planning, startup, expansion, and marketing in Canada.

Indigenous Growth Fund

A $150M fund providing capital to AFIs enabling larger loans and flexible terms for Indigenous businesses that may not qualify for traditional bank financing.

Community Economic Development

Programs like CORP and the Strategic Partnerships Initiative funding community-owned businesses, infrastructure, and capacity building on First Nations lands.

Foundation and Private Sector Support

Beyond government programs, foundations and corporations provide targeted funding for Indigenous economic empowerment.

Foundation Grants

Organizations like the First Nations Development Institute, the Christensen Fund, and the Canadian Council for Aboriginal Business provide grants for Indigenous economic development, cultural preservation, and community capacity building.

Corporate Procurement Programs

Major corporations operate Indigenous procurement targets and supplier diversity programs. Combined with mentorship and capacity building, these create sustainable revenue streams for Indigenous businesses.

Frequently Asked Questions

What grants are available for Indigenous-owned businesses?

Indigenous entrepreneurs can access dedicated federal programs like Indigenous Services Canada grants, Aboriginal Business Canada, the Aboriginal Entrepreneurship Program, and the Indigenous Growth Fund. Provincial programs, Aboriginal Financial Institutions (AFIs), and private foundations also provide significant funding.

How does the Aboriginal Entrepreneurship Program work?

The Aboriginal Entrepreneurship Program (AEP) provides non-repayable contributions up to $99,999 for business planning, startup, expansion, and marketing through a network of Aboriginal Financial Institutions. It supports Indigenous entrepreneurs with business plans, equity contributions, and aftercare services.

What is the Indigenous Growth Fund?

The Indigenous Growth Fund is a $150M fund providing capital to Aboriginal Financial Institutions (AFIs), which then lend to Indigenous businesses. It enables larger loan sizes and more flexible terms for Indigenous entrepreneurs who may not qualify for traditional bank financing.

Are there grants for Indigenous businesses in the USA?

Yes, US programs include the Indian Affairs Office of Indian Economic Development grants, SBA 8(a) Business Development for Native Americans, the Native American Business Development Institute (NABDI), and various tribal economic development grants.

Can Indigenous communities apply for economic development grants?

Yes, Indigenous communities and bands can apply for community economic development grants including the Community Opportunity Readiness Program (CORP), Strategic Partnerships Initiative, and regional development grants. These fund infrastructure, community businesses, and capacity building.
