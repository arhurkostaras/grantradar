Frequently Asked Questions

What grants are available for agriculture and farming?

Agricultural grants include USDA programs (Conservation Stewardship, EQIP, Value-Added Producer Grants), Agriculture and Agri-Food Canada's CAP programs, EU Common Agricultural Policy funding, and state/provincial programs for farm innovation and food processing.

How do I apply for USDA agricultural grants?

USDA grants are available through NIFA, FSA, NRCS, and Rural Development. Applications typically require SAM.gov registration, a project narrative, budget, and supporting documents. Programs like EQIP and CSP are applied for through your local USDA Service Center.

Are there grants for agri-food technology companies?

Yes, agtech companies can access innovation grants including USDA SBIR, AAFC AgriInnovate, Innovate UK Farming Innovation, and various state/provincial programs. Precision agriculture, vertical farming, alternative proteins, and agricultural AI are heavily funded.

What grants exist for food processing businesses?

Food processors can access USDA Value-Added Producer Grants, Canada's CAP AgriProcessing, state MEP funding, and regional economic development grants. Programs support equipment modernization, food safety, product development, and export expansion.

Can organic farmers get special grants?

Yes, organic farmers have access to USDA Organic Certification Cost Share, EQIP Organic Initiative, and Transition to Organic Research grants. Canada offers Organic Farming Innovation grants through various provincial programs. Many general agriculture grants also give preference to organic practices.
