Types of Nonprofit Funding

Nonprofits can access diverse funding streams from private foundations, government agencies, and corporate partners.

Foundation Grants

Private foundations like Gates, Ford, Rockefeller, MacArthur, and thousands of community foundations fund nonprofit programs across education, health, environment, arts, and social justice.

Government Programs

Federal, state, and municipal governments fund nonprofits through competitive grants for social services, community development, healthcare, education, and environmental conservation.

Corporate Giving

Corporate foundations and CSR programs provide grants, sponsorships, and in-kind support. Programs from Google, Microsoft, Walmart, and others fund nonprofits in their focus areas.

Grant Application Best Practices

Strengthen your nonprofit grant applications with these proven strategies.

1

Mission Alignment

Only apply to grants where your mission directly aligns with the funder's priorities. Quality over quantity wins.

2

Impact Metrics

Quantify your outcomes. Funders want to see measurable impact data from past programs and realistic projections.

3

Strong Financials

Present clear, audited financials with diverse revenue streams. Funders prefer organizations with sustainable financial management.

4

Relationship Building

Cultivate funder relationships before applying. Attend events, provide updates, and engage with program officers.

Frequently Asked Questions

What types of grants are available for nonprofits?

Nonprofits can access foundation grants (Gates, Ford, Rockefeller), government program grants (federal and state/provincial), corporate giving programs, community foundation grants, and international development funding. Grant amounts range from $1,000 community grants to multi-million dollar program funding.

How do nonprofits find grant opportunities?

Nonprofits can find grants through GrantRadar's AI-powered matching, foundation directories, government portals like Grants.gov and Canada.ca, community foundation websites, and professional networks. GrantRadar aggregates all these sources and matches them to your mission, size, and location.

What do foundations look for in nonprofit grant applications?

Foundations evaluate mission alignment, organizational capacity, measurable outcomes, financial health, board governance, and sustainability plans. Strong applications demonstrate clear impact metrics, realistic budgets, evidence of community need, and a track record of program delivery.

Can new nonprofits apply for grants?

Yes, but options may be more limited. Many foundations require 1-3 years of operating history and audited financials. However, fiscal sponsorship allows new nonprofits to receive grants through an established organization. Community foundations and some government programs specifically support emerging nonprofits.

What is the difference between restricted and unrestricted grants?

Restricted grants must be used for a specific project or purpose defined by the funder. Unrestricted (or general operating) grants can be used for any organizational expense including salaries, rent, and overhead. Unrestricted funding is highly valued but harder to obtain.
