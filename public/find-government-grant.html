Frequently Asked Questions

What are government grants?

Government grants are non-repayable funds provided by federal, state, provincial, or municipal governments to support businesses, nonprofits, and research institutions. Unlike loans, grants do not need to be paid back.

Who is eligible for government grants?

Eligibility varies by program but typically includes small and medium businesses, startups, nonprofits, research institutions, and Indigenous-owned organizations. Requirements may include industry sector, location, business size, and project type.

How do I apply for a government grant?

Most government grants require an online application through the granting agency's portal. You will typically need a business plan, financial statements, project description, and budget. GrantRadar provides direct links to application portals.

How long does it take to receive a government grant?

Processing times vary widely from 4 weeks to 6 months depending on the program. Some programs have faster turnarounds, while large federal programs may take several months for review and approval.

Can I apply for multiple government grants at once?

Yes, in most cases you can apply for multiple government grants simultaneously, as long as the programs do not explicitly prohibit stacking. However, some grants may require disclosure of other funding received for the same project.
