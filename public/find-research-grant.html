Research Grant FAQs

What types of research grants are available?

Research grants include science council funding (NSERC, NSF, UKRI), foundation grants (Wellcome, Gates), industry partnerships, postdoctoral fellowships, and international programs like Horizon Europe.

Can private companies apply for research grants?

Yes, many research grants are available to private companies through programs like SR&ED, R&D Tax Credits, SBIR/STTR, and Horizon Europe.

How competitive are research grants?

Success rates vary widely. Major science council grants typically have 15-25% success rates. GrantRadar helps identify the best-fit opportunities.

What is the SR&ED tax credit?

SR&ED is Canada's largest R&D incentive, providing tax credits of 15-35% on eligible R&D expenditures for Canadian companies of all sizes.

Can international researchers apply for grants in other countries?

Many research grants welcome international applicants, especially for collaborative projects. Programs like Horizon Europe and bilateral agreements facilitate international research partnerships.
