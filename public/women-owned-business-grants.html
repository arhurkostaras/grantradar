Programs Supporting Women Entrepreneurs

Governments and foundations worldwide have created dedicated funding streams to close the gender gap in business ownership and entrepreneurship.

Government Programs

Canada's Women Entrepreneurship Strategy ($6B+), SBA Women's Business Centers (USA), and Innovate UK Women in Innovation awards provide direct government support.

Foundation Grants

The Cartier Women's Initiative, Amber Grant ($10K monthly), IFundWomen, and the Tory Burch Foundation provide non-dilutive grants and mentorship for women founders.

Accelerators & Competitions

Women-focused accelerators like SheEO, Female Founders Alliance, and Springboard Enterprises offer equity-free grants, mentorship, and investor introductions.

Frequently Asked Questions

What grants are available for women-owned businesses?

Women entrepreneurs can access dedicated programs including SBA Women's Business Centers (USA), Women Entrepreneurship Strategy (Canada), Innovate UK Women in Innovation, and foundation grants from organizations like the Cartier Women's Initiative, Amber Grant Foundation, and IFundWomen.

How do I qualify as a women-owned business for grants?

Most programs require that the business be at least 51% owned, controlled, and operated by one or more women. In the USA, WBENC certification or SBA Women-Owned Small Business designation provides official recognition. In Canada, WBE Canada certification is the standard.

What is the Amber Grant for women?

The Amber Grant Foundation awards monthly grants of $10,000 to women-owned businesses, plus an additional $25,000 year-end grant. It was established in honor of Amber Wigdahl. Applications are simple and open to all women-owned businesses.

Are there government grants specifically for women entrepreneurs?

Yes, many governments operate women-focused programs. Canada's Women Entrepreneurship Strategy provides $6B+ in funding. The USA offers SBA Women's Business Centers and SCORE mentoring. The UK runs Women in Innovation awards through Innovate UK.

Can women-owned businesses access general grants too?

Absolutely. Women-owned businesses qualify for all general business grants in addition to women-specific programs. Many general grants give preference or bonus points to diverse applicants including women-owned businesses. GrantRadar matches you to both.
