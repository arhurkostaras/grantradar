Key UK Funding Bodies

The UK government supports business growth through several key agencies and programmes offering grants, tax relief, and equity co-investment.

Innovate UK

The UK's innovation agency providing Smart Grants up to 500K, Catalyst programmes, and Knowledge Transfer Partnerships for businesses developing cutting-edge products and services.

HMRC R&D Tax Relief

Claim tax credits of up to 186% enhanced deduction on qualifying research and development costs. Loss-making SMEs can receive a 10% cash credit on surrenderable losses.

British Business Bank

Government-owned development bank offering Start Up Loans, the Future Fund, Regional Angel programmes, and co-investment funds for high-growth businesses.

Frequently Asked Questions

What grants are available for UK small businesses?

UK small businesses can access Innovate UK Smart Grants for innovation projects, HMRC R&D Tax Relief for research expenditure, British Business Bank programs, regional Local Enterprise Partnership grants, and sector-specific funds like the Clean Growth Fund and Digital Growth Programme.

How does Innovate UK funding work?

Innovate UK provides competitive grants through programmes like Smart Grants (up to 500K for game-changing innovations), Catalyst programmes for specific sectors, and the Knowledge Transfer Partnership. Applications are assessed on innovation, team capability, market potential, and project plan.

What is the UK R&D Tax Relief scheme?

HMRC R&D Tax Relief allows UK companies to claim tax credits on qualifying R&D expenditure. SMEs can claim up to 186% enhanced deduction or a 10% credit if loss-making. The RDEC scheme for larger companies provides a 20% above-the-line credit on qualifying costs.

Can UK startups get government grants?

Yes, UK startups can apply for Innovate UK grants, the Start Up Loans programme (up to 25K per co-founder), Enterprise Investment Scheme support, and regional innovation grants. Many programs have no minimum trading history requirement.

Are there grants for UK businesses post-Brexit?

Yes, the UK has replaced EU Structural Funds with the UK Shared Prosperity Fund and Levelling Up Fund. Additionally, Horizon Europe association allows UK researchers to access EU research grants. Innovate UK continues to expand domestic innovation funding.
