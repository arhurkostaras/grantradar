Federal Grant Programs

The Australian Government funds business innovation through AusIndustry, the ARC, CSIRO, and sector-specific agencies.

R&D Tax Incentive

Refundable 43.5% tax offset for SMEs with turnover under $20M conducting eligible research and experimental development activities in Australia.

EMDG Export Grants

Reimburses up to 50% of eligible export promotion expenses for Australian exporters, covering marketing, trade fairs, and overseas visits up to $150K.

Accelerating Commercialisation

Matched grants up to $1M for entrepreneurs and businesses to commercialise novel products, processes, and services in domestic and international markets.

Frequently Asked Questions

What business grants are available in Australia?

Australia offers numerous grants through AusIndustry including the R&D Tax Incentive, Export Market Development Grants (EMDG), Entrepreneurs' Programme, and Accelerating Commercialisation. State governments also provide grants through programs like LaunchVic, NSW Boosting Business Innovation, and QLD Advance Queensland.

How does the Australian R&D Tax Incentive work?

The R&D Tax Incentive provides a tax offset for eligible R&D activities. Companies with aggregated turnover under $20M receive a refundable 43.5% tax offset. Larger companies receive a non-refundable offset of their corporate tax rate plus an 8.5% premium. Claims are made through AusIndustry and the ATO.

What is the Export Market Development Grants (EMDG) program?

EMDG is an Australian Government financial assistance program for aspiring and current exporters. It reimburses up to 50% of eligible export promotion expenses above $5,000, with a maximum of $150,000 per grant. Eligible activities include overseas marketing, trade fairs, and market research.

Can Australian startups get government grants?

Yes, Australian startups can access Accelerating Commercialisation grants up to $1M, CSIRO Kick-Start vouchers for research collaboration, state-based startup funds like LaunchVic, and the ARC Linkage program for university-industry partnerships. Many programs target companies under 3 years old.

Are there grants for Australian exporters?

Yes, the EMDG program specifically supports exporters. Additionally, Austrade provides market entry support, the International Business Grant (in some states) funds export activities, and Export Finance Australia offers financial solutions for exporters including loans and guarantees.
