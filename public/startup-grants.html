Why Grants Over VC?

Non-dilutive funding lets you build your product, validate your market, and grow your team without giving away equity.

Preserve Equity

Grants require zero equity dilution. Keep full ownership and control while funding early-stage R&D and product development.

Validate Technology

Government grant awards serve as third-party validation of your technology and business model, making future fundraising easier.

No Repayment

Most grants are non-repayable contributions. Unlike loans or convertible notes, you never pay the money back regardless of outcome.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are non-dilutive grants for startups?

Non-dilutive grants are funding you receive without giving up equity in your company. Unlike venture capital, grants do not require you to surrender ownership or board seats. Government innovation programs, SBIR/STTR awards, and foundation grants are all non-dilutive, meaning founders retain 100% of their company.

Why should startups pursue grants instead of VC funding?

Grants preserve founder equity, have no repayment obligation, validate your technology for future investors, and extend runway without dilution. Many successful startups combine grants with VC funding — using grants for early R&D and VC for scaling.

What types of startup grants exist?

Startup grants include government innovation programs (SBIR, IRAP, Innovate UK), accelerator grants, pitch competition prizes, foundation funding, corporate innovation challenges, and university commercialization grants. Amounts range from $5K pitch prizes to $1.5M SBIR Phase II awards.

How do startups qualify for government grants?

Most startup grants require incorporation in the funding country, a technology innovation or R&D component, a qualified team, and a clear commercialization plan. Some programs require minimum operating history (6-24 months) while others accept pre-revenue companies.

Can pre-revenue startups get grants?

Yes, many programs specifically target pre-revenue startups. SBIR Phase I, Innovate UK Smart Grants, NRC IRAP, and most accelerator grants accept companies without revenue. The key requirements are usually a viable technology concept, a strong team, and a clear path to market.
