Indigenous Grant FAQs

What grants are available for Indigenous businesses?

Indigenous businesses can access dedicated government programs, Aboriginal business development funds, community economic development grants, and capacity building programs.

Do I need to be registered as an Indigenous business?

Requirements vary by program. In Canada, many programs require membership in an Indigenous community. In Australia, Supply Nation certification may be needed. In the USA, tribal enrollment or BIA certification is common.

Can Indigenous businesses also apply for general grants?

Yes, Indigenous-owned businesses are eligible for both Indigenous-specific and general business grants. Many general programs give priority to Indigenous applicants.

What sectors are covered by Indigenous grants?

Indigenous grants cover all sectors including natural resources, tourism, technology, agriculture, arts and culture, construction, clean energy, and professional services.

Are there grants for Indigenous youth entrepreneurs?

Yes, several programs specifically target Indigenous youth entrepreneurs, including Futurpreneur Indigenous Entrepreneur stream in Canada and First Nations Enterprise Scholarships in Australia.
