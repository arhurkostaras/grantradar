Top Federal Funding Agencies

The US federal government distributes grant funding through dozens of agencies, each with specific program areas and eligibility requirements.

NIH — National Institutes of Health

The largest public funder of biomedical research in the world, awarding over $40 billion annually in research grants across 27 institutes.

NSF — National Science Foundation

Funds fundamental research and education across all non-medical fields of science and engineering with an annual budget exceeding $9 billion.

DOE — Department of Energy

Funds clean energy, advanced manufacturing, and nuclear research. ARPA-E provides high-risk, high-reward energy technology grants.

USDA — Agriculture

Grants for rural development, agriculture research, food safety, and conservation through programs like SBIR, NIFA, and Rural Energy.

SBIR/STTR Programs

America's flagship small business innovation programs across 11 agencies. Phase I: $50K-$275K. Phase II: $500K-$1.5M. Non-dilutive.

DoD — Department of Defense

Defense research grants through DARPA, ONR, AFOSR, and ARO. Strong SBIR program for dual-use technologies and defense applications.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Grants.gov and how does it work?

Grants.gov is the US government's centralized portal for finding and applying to federal grant opportunities. It lists funding from over 26 federal agencies including NIH, NSF, DOE, and USDA. Organizations can search, save, and apply to grants directly through the portal after registering with SAM.gov.

How many federal grants are available in the USA?

At any given time, Grants.gov lists thousands of active federal funding opportunities totaling hundreds of billions of dollars annually. The exact number fluctuates as new grants are posted and deadlines pass. GrantRadar tracks all active opportunities and matches them to your organization's profile.

Do I need to register on SAM.gov to apply for federal grants?

Yes, all organizations applying for US federal grants must register in the System for Award Management (SAM.gov) and obtain a Unique Entity Identifier (UEI). Registration is free but can take 2-4 weeks, so register early before grant deadlines.

Can small businesses apply for federal grants?

Yes, the US government offers extensive small business grant programs including SBIR (Small Business Innovation Research) and STTR (Small Business Technology Transfer) across 11 federal agencies. The SBA also provides resources and some direct funding for small businesses.

What are SBIR and STTR grants?

SBIR and STTR are competitive programs that provide non-dilutive funding to small businesses for technology innovation. Phase I awards are typically $50K-$275K for feasibility studies. Phase II awards range from $500K-$1.5M for full R&D. Eleven federal agencies participate in the SBIR program.
